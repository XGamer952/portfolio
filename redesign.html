Responsive Redesign

Transforming poorly-designed websites with a responsive new coat of paint.

The original website for Pacific Northwest X-Ray Inc. had a layout stuck in the early 2000s: cramped content, inconsistent alignment, low color contrast, and a cluttered shopping interface.

I chose to redesign it to focus on clean hierarchy, mobile responsiveness, modern accessibility, and a patient-friendly shopping experience. You can view the final site here.

Identifying Issues

  • Small fonts and very low color contrast (especially gold text on white)
  • No responsive layout—mobile view was completely broken
  • Redundant buttons (e.g., "Browse Products" and “Equipment” go to the same place)
  • Inconsistent spacing and alignment of sections, images, and typography
  • Dense blocks of text and too many competing focal points

Visual Style Guide

Based on a redesigned layout using Figma

I created a style guide to guide the redesign, with a custom logo, updated colors, consistent font scale, and button states. These established hierarchy and clarity across screen sizes.
